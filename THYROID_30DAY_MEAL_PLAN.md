## SIMPLE RECIPES (15-30 minutes)

### BREAKFAST SCRAMBLE
**Ingredients:**
- 3 eggs
- 1 cup spinach
- 1/2 cup mushrooms
- 1/4 onion
- 1 tbsp ghee
- Sea salt & pepper

**Instructions:**
1. Heat ghee in pan over medium heat
2. Saut√© mushrooms & onion 3 minutes
3. Add spinach, cook until wilted (2 min)
4. Whisk eggs in bowl
5. Pour eggs over vegetables
6. Stir occasionally until fully cooked (3-4 min)
7. Season to taste

**Calories:** ~220 | **Protein:** 18g | **Carbs:** 8g | **Fat:** 14g

---

### GRILLED SALMON WITH LEMON
**Ingredients:**
- 5 oz salmon fillet
- 1 tbsp olive oil
- 1/2 lemon
- Sea salt & pepper
- Fresh herbs (dill or oregano)

**Instructions:**
1. Preheat oven to 400¬∞F
2. Place salmon on parchment paper
3. Drizzle olive oil, squeeze lemon
4. Sprinkle herbs, salt, pepper
5. Bake 12-15 minutes until cooked through
6. Serve with roasted vegetables

**Calories:** ~280 | **Protein:** 35g | **Carbs:** 2g | **Fat:** 16g

---

### GROUND BEEF & VEGETABLE STIR-FRY
**Ingredients:**
- 4 oz grass-fed ground beef
- 1 cup mixed vegetables (broccoli, peppers, snap peas)
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tbsp sesame oil
- 1 tbsp tamari (gluten-free soy)
- 1/2 cup rice or cauliflower rice

**Instructions:**
1. Heat sesame oil in large pan/wok
2. Add ground beef, break up while cooking (5 min)
3. Add garlic, cook 1 minute
4. Add vegetables, stir-fry 5-7 minutes
5. Add tamari, toss well
6. Serve over rice
7. Ready to eat in 20 minutes!

**Calories:** ~380 | **Protein:** 28g | **Carbs:** 32g | **Fat:** 15g

---

### ROASTED VEGETABLES
**Ingredients:**
- 2 cups mixed vegetables (broccoli, Brussels sprouts, asparagus, carrots)
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- Sea salt & pepper

**Instructions:**
1. Preheat oven to 425¬∞F

6. Serve hot

**Make extra:** Roasts keep 4-5 days in fridge. Great for side dishes!

## COOKING TIPS FOR THYROID HEALTH

### Selenium-Rich Cooking
- Cook fish 2-3x per week
- Use Brazil nuts as garnish
- Include eggs at breakfast

### Avoid Goitrogen Issues
- **Always cook** cruciferous vegetables (kale, broccoli, cauliflower)
- Raw kale smoothies = not ideal
- Roasted kale chips = perfect!

### Maximize Nutrient Absorption
- Cook with healthy fats (olive oil, ghee)
- Combine iron + Vitamin C (beef + lemon)
- Space minerals (don't take calcium with iron)

### Time Your Meals
- Breakfast within 1 hour of waking
- 4-5 hours between meals
- Dinner 3 hours before bed
- Coffee 90 minutes after waking

## SHOPPING SHORTCUTS

**If short on time:**
- Buy pre-cut vegetables (costs more, saves time)

- Buy eggs (cheapest protein)
- Buy frozen fish (cheaper than fresh)
- Buy in bulk when possible

## TROUBLESHOOTING

**Q: I don't like fish**
A: Get omega-3s from grass-fed beef, eggs, nuts. Take fish oil supplement.

**Q: What about vegetarian?**
A: Very difficult for thyroid. But can use: lots of eggs, dairy (if tolerated), nuts, seeds, beans.

**Q: How much should I eat?**
A: Eat until satisfied (not stuffed). Listen to hunger cues. Most people need 1,600-2,000 calories.

**Q: Can I eat fruit?**
A: Yes! Berries, apples, citrus (especially with meals). Avoid dried fruit & fruit juice.

**Q: What about dairy?**
A: If tolerated, organic grassfed is best. Many thyroid patients do better off dairy. Try elimination.
