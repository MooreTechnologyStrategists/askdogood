# AskDoGood Competitor And Partnership Brief

## AskDoGood's Competitive Lane
AskDoGood sits between clinic, content brand, and community platform.

It overlaps with lifestyle medicine, chronic-disease education, and wellness coaching, but it is more culturally grounded, more founder-led, and more practical for everyday implementation than most institutional competitors.

The strongest positioning move is to own this lane:
AskDoGood is the bridge between diagnosis and daily life.

## Competitor Map

### Local DMV
- GW Lifestyle Medicine Clinic
  - Strength: hospital-backed lifestyle medicine, clinical authority, prevention and chronic-disease framing
  - Weakness: less personal, less culturally distinct, less founder-led warmth
- University of Maryland Medical Center Well on Your Way
  - Strength: academic and institutional trust
  - Weakness: likely less differentiated storytelling and lower lifestyle-brand energy
- Change for Life Wellness & Aesthetics
  - Strength: transformation-oriented female audience and local visibility
  - Weakness: less depth in chronic-illness education and practical daily systems
- Capital Integrative Health
  - Strength: integrative-clinic framing by name
  - Weakness: current web visibility appears weak or inactive
- Eze Health Center
  - Limited accessible public information during research
- Vibrant Healthcare
  - Limited accessible public information during research

### National Or International
- Mayo Clinic Integrative Medicine and Health
  - Strength: national authority and clinical infrastructure
  - Weakness: slow-moving partnership path and low cultural intimacy
- Euphoria Retreat
  - Strength: premium transformation and aspirational wellness branding
  - Weakness: destination-luxury model is far from everyday community access
- Professional Nutrition Services / Holistic Health Center
  - Strength: behavior change, food guidance, nutrition counseling
  - Weakness: narrower brand range than AskDoGood
- Horizon Beuil
  - Limited accessible public information during research
- International Institute of Holistic Health
  - Limited accessible public information during research

### Educational And Community
- Chronic Disease Self-Management Program / Self-Management Resource Center
  - Strength: structured workshops and evidence-based self-management model
  - Weakness: less brand warmth, less culture-rooted delivery, less commerce flexibility

## What Makes AskDoGood Different
- Lived experience instead of institution-only authority
- Stronger relevance for Black and Brown wellness journeys
- A broader model that includes healing, routine, community, and economic stability
- Lower-friction digital access through content, Gumroad tools, and guided offers
- A clearer bridge between education and implementation

## Recommended Public Mission
AskDoGood helps people heal better, live smarter, and earn stronger through practical wellness, self-advocacy, and everyday stability.

## Recommended Positioning Pillars
- Stability-first healing
- Culture-rooted wellness
- Self-advocacy with structure

## Best Collaboration Targets

### 1. GW Lifestyle Medicine Clinic
Why it fits:
- Strong local credibility
- Aligned language around food, movement, stress, sleep, and prevention
- Clear complement to AskDoGood's everyday implementation angle

Low-friction pitch:
Co-host a webinar or Q and A translating lifestyle medicine into a practical weekly routine for busy DMV women.

### 2. University of Maryland Medical Center Well on Your Way
Why it fits:
- Maryland-local relevance
- Institutional trust
- Good fit for community-facing education or handouts

Low-friction pitch:
Offer a co-branded meal-prep guide, symptom-and-routine planner, or community workshop support resource.

### 3. Self-Management Resource Center / CDSMP
Why it fits:
- Strong alignment with chronic-disease self-management
- Practical, workshop-friendly model
- Good fit for a culturally adapted companion resource

Low-friction pitch:
Create an AskDoGood companion guide focused on what to do between sessions.

### 4. Change for Life Wellness & Aesthetics
Why it fits:
- Female-centered audience
- Local DC presence
- Strong transformation and self-investment framing

Low-friction pitch:
Co-host a live conversation on inside-out wellness: food, stress, sleep, and confidence.

### 5. Professional Nutrition Services
Why it fits:
- Practical food guidance overlap
- Useful for GI, inflammation, and behavior-change content
- Credible collaboration without requiring a hospital-scale process

Low-friction pitch:
Create a grocery-store walkthrough, label-reading event, or practical nutrition Q and A.

## Outreach Angle
Core line:
You treat the patient. AskDoGood helps translate healing into everyday routines people can actually keep.

Alternative line for community partners:
AskDoGood modernizes wellness education with real-life language, practical tools, and stronger trust with the people programs often struggle to reach.

## Guardrails
To stay partner-friendly and medically safe:
- Avoid cure language
- Avoid promising medication discontinuation
- Position products as education, support, routines, and self-advocacy tools
- Keep testimonials framed as individual experiences, not guaranteed outcomes
