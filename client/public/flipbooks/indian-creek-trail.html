The Flipbook

8 Frames from a Beautiful Day

Greenbelt → Riverdale → Hyattsville → Cottage City → Bladensburg — all on foot, all in one morning.

Scene 01 01 / 08
The smile says everything
Before my thyroid was removed, color looked different. Muted. Like someone had turned the saturation down on my whole life. This morning? Everything was vivid. That smile is not just a good day. It is a comeback.
Comeback Season
Scene 02 02 / 08
Five cities, one trail
Riverdale. Hyattsville. Cottage City. Bladensburg. Greenbelt. I walked between them all and finally understood how they connect. From ground level, with good company, the map starts to make sense in a way Google never could.
Perspective
Scene 03 03 / 08
Chalk on the asphalt
Someone got on their knees and wrote this for every survivor who walks this path. April is Sexual Assault Awareness Month. That kind of quiet, anonymous advocacy? That is what community actually looks like.
Awareness
Scene 04 04 / 08
PG Parks does not miss
Manicured edges. Exercise stations. Maintained bridges. This is what investment in public space looks like. Parks and Rec gave us something beautiful and I want people to know it exists.
Community Love
Scene 05 05 / 08
The creek I never knew was here
I have driven these roads for years. I had no idea this was tucked behind all of it. Indian Creek moving quietly through the trees, reflecting the sky back at you like it has been waiting to be seen.
Discovery
Scene 06 06 / 08
Nature does not ask permission
A sandbar curved right through the middle of the waterway like the creek just decided to rearrange itself. Raw, unhurried, unbothered. I needed to see something that was fully itself that morning.
Urban Nature
Scene 07 07 / 08
Rust and roots and real life
Graffiti on old concrete pillars, roots pushing through rock, water doing what water does. This is not pretty in the curated sense. It is honest. And on a day when I felt genuinely alive again, honest hit hard.
Raw Beauty
Scene 08 08 / 08
Goal was 5. We did 5.7.
12,840 steps. Before my thyroidectomy, a day this full would have left me hollow. I walked 5.7 miles feeling good, feeling present, feeling safe. That number is not just a step count. It is proof.
The Proof
The world did not get more beautiful. I just got well enough to see it again.
